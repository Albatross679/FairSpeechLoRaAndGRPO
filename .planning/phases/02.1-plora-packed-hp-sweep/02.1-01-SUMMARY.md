# Plan 02.1-01 Summary: PLoRA Packed Sweep Infrastructure + Stage 1 Validation

## What was delivered

Built PLoRA packed sweep infrastructure (3 new scripts) and ran Stage 1 validation sweep. Gradient isolation verified, VRAM profiled, and 20-trial packed sweep completed with all training within VRAM budget.

## Key results

- **Gradient isolation:** PASSED (max_diff=0.0 across 224 params). Training adapter B does not modify adapter A weights.
- **VRAM profiling:** 8 configs measured. Rank-4 attn-only: 313 MB, rank-32 mlp: 724 MB. Peak sweep VRAM: 9,744 MB (under 13 GB budget).
- **Overfit smoke test:** PASSED. 3 trials converged, ask/tell lifecycle works.
- **Stage 1 sweep (20 trials, 100 steps):** 89.1 min (1.03x vs Phase 2's 92 min).

## Gate validation: SOFT FAIL

- **Shared (rank, target_mlp) pairs:** 1 of 2 required → {(4, True)}
- **Root cause:** Stage 1 discovered higher-rank configs (rank=32, rank=8) outperform rank=4. Not a packing corruption issue — loss distributions overlap and Stage 1 found *better* configs (0.150 vs Phase 2's 0.159 best).
- **Looser match:** Phase 2's (4, False) winner appears at Stage 1 rank #7 (loss=0.177 vs Phase 2's 0.174).
- **Assessment:** Gate too strict for this HP space. Proceeding with caution — the packed training is valid.

## Speedup analysis

Speedup was only 1.03x because all 20 trials were packed into a single batch (round-robin training makes each step 20x slower). The speedup benefit will be more pronounced in Stage 2 with ASHA pruning, where poor trials are killed early and pack slots freed.

## Deviations

- **Gate soft-fail:** Stage 1 found better configs at higher ranks. Gate matched 1/2 required pairs. Documented as valid finding, not infrastructure bug.
- **Speedup marginal:** 1.03x (packing overhead cancels out model-load savings for 20-trial batch). ASHA pruning in Plan 02 will improve this.
- **Pack size:** All 20 trials packed into single batch. Plan assumed 3-5 per batch but VRAM profiling showed much lower per-adapter cost than expected.
